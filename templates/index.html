Explore Career Streams

After 10th grade, students can choose from various career streams:

    Science

    • Science stream is tailored for students interested in subjects like Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and Mathematics (PCBM).
    • It includes in-depth study of natural phenomena, scientific principles, and experimentation.
    • Subjects typically covered include Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics, and elective subjects like Computer Science or Biotechnology.
    • This stream opens up pathways to careers in medicine, engineering, research, biotechnology, pharmacy, and more.

    Commerce

    • Commerce stream is ideal for students interested in business, finance, and economics.
    • It includes subjects like Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics, and Mathematics.
    • Students learn about financial transactions, business management, economics principles, and taxation.
    • This stream prepares students for careers in accounting, finance, banking, entrepreneurship, management, and commerce-related fields.

    Arts

    • Arts stream is for students with a passion for humanities, languages, literature, and social sciences.
    • It includes subjects like History, Geography, Political Science, Sociology, Psychology, Economics, and Languages (English, Regional Languages).
    • Students delve into the study of society, culture, history, literature, and human behavior.
    • This stream offers opportunities in fields such as journalism, teaching, civil services, social work, literature, fine arts, and more.

    Engineering

    • Engineering stream focuses on the application of scientific principles to design, develop, and maintain structures, machines, systems, and processes.
    • It includes subjects like Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and specialized branches such as Mechanical, Civil, Electrical, Computer Science, and Electronics Engineering.
    • Students learn problem-solving, critical thinking, and technical skills required for various engineering disciplines.
    • This stream leads to careers in fields like aerospace, automotive, civil, computer, electrical, and mechanical engineering, among others.

    Vocational Courses

    • Vocational courses provide practical skills and training for specific trades or industries.
    • It includes courses like ITI (Industrial Training Institute), Diploma in Engineering, Polytechnic courses, and vocational training programs in sectors such as hospitality, healthcare, automotive, construction, and more.
    • Students gain hands-on experience and specialized skills relevant to their chosen field.
    • This stream offers direct entry into the workforce or further education in specialized areas.
